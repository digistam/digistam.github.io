System Operation Cost Calculation

Based on the following data, you need to calculate the approximate cost of recovery of the system operion per year

      The cost of a new hard drive is $ 300
      The chance of hard drive failure is 1/3
      The recovery specialist earns $ 10 / hour
      Restore the OS and software to the new hard disk is 10 hours
      Restore the database from the last backup to the new hard disk is 4 hours
      Assume the EF = 1 (100%)

Explanation

      AV (Asset Value) = $300 + (14 * $10) = $440 - This represents the total cost to replace a single asset, including the price of a new hard drive and the recovery specialist's time. The recover process involves 10 hours for restoring the OS and software and 4 hours for restoring the database, multiplied by the recovery specialist's hourly rate
      SLE (Single Loss Expectancy) = AV * EF (Exposure Factor) = $440 * 1 = $440 - This is the expected loss for a single event, which is the asset value multiplied by the exposure factor (EF), assumed to be 100%
      ARO (Annual Rate of Occurence) = 1/3 - This means the probability of the event occurring in one year is 1/3, based on the fact that it happens once every three years
      ALE (Annual Loss Expectancy) = SLE * ARO = 0.33 * $440 = $145.2 - This represents the expected annual loss, calculated by multiplying the single loss expectancy by the annual rate of occurence
